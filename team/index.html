The people behind the libraries

Team

We maintain the libraries, review each other's code, and keep up with the specs. New contributors are welcome.

TB

Tobias Brox

@tobixen

The original. Tobias started building CalDAV tooling in 2013 because he needed it, not because it was a project. That work became calendar-cli, then python-caldav, then plann. Over a decade later he still maintains all three, and knows where every server quirk is buried.

python-caldav plann calendaring-client
NK

Nicco Kunzmann

@niccokunzmann

Without Nicco, most of this wouldn't exist as a coordinated effort. He maintains icalendar, recurring-ical-events, and open-web-calendar, reviews code across all packages, and has been the one keeping everything aligned — technically and organizationally. He also brought most of this team together.

icalendar recurring-ical-events open-web-calendar
SB

Sashank Bhamidi

@SashankBhamidi LinkedIn

Sashank is the CEO of BizCred and does open source on the side. He maintains icalendar and icalendar-anonymizer, and built the JMAP implementation in python-caldav from scratch.

icalendar calendaring-jmap icalendar-anonymizer
AH

Abe Hanoka

@abe-101

Abe runs actual production services on these libraries — MergeCal.org and MyHebrewDates.com — which means he regularly hits bugs that only show up at scale or with unusual calendar data. That's been genuinely useful. He maintains python-mergecal and django-icalendar, and is also a maintainer of dj-stripe.

mergecal django-icalendar
SP

Steve Piercy

@stevepiercy

Good documentation is the difference between a library people use and one they abandon after an hour. Steve takes that seriously. He leads docs for this project and for Plone, is bringing iCalendar support to Plone's event content types, and maintains Sphinx tooling that several projects in this space depend on.

Documentation Sphinx tooling
